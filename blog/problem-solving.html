    Solving a simple Problem

    It can be disheartening to encounter a seemingly simple problem and it blocking you. Here's how you can over come:
    While attempting to solve a problem where I had to make all of a sentence lower case so I could test it, I found difficulty in changing everything to lower case and then making 2 letters uppercase again (for the sentence).
    To begin, I wrote a sentence on paper describing what I wanted to do. Since I was tired, I couldn't think properly so I asked for help in the discord and got directed to an exercise to help.
    Throughout the whole process, I felt really frustrated because I knew I should have been able to solve it but I struggled for quite some time.
    What I learnt is that I should really have taken a step back and had a walk and a breather to calm down before going back and trying again.

    Elgantly solving a problem:

    While trying to complete a problem where I had to add a new property to a group of objects in an array, I had to figure out how to count to the right object and add the right name. I tried many different ways of doing it.
    To solve it, I wrote some pseudo code so I knew what I needed it to do. Then I talked it through with someone and had a break. When I went back to the problem, I found it much easier to overcome it. I tried researching a method I thought would work and used it to solve my problem
    I wasn't as frustrated as I was before as I felt I had some leads and would figure this out. When I solved it, I was elated
    I cemented the idea of taking breaks when I was getting frustrated and talking through my issue. The solution came a lot easier

    Problem Solving techniques

    I'm confident with a few problem solving techniques such as pseudo-code and Googling. These are my go-to when I first encounter a problem. Trying things I'm fairly confident with but I need to have a decent idea of what I can do first. Anything that involves talking it through or asking for help is something I struggle with as I don't want to feel silly or stupid. I'm not very good at relfecting as it requires me to slow down which I am not good at

    Asking for help

    I am often reluctant to ask for help because I want to be able to solve a problem myself before talking to anyone. I often feel like asking for help is admitting defeat (only for myself, I never judge others for getting help) and so I only do it if I'm very stuck on a problem
